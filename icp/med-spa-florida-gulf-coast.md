# ICP: Med Spa — Florida Gulf Coast

## The One-Line Pitch
Your reviews are doing the work. Your website isn't. And your competitors are about to figure out AI search before you do.

---

## Who They Are
- **Business type:** Medical aesthetics / med spa
- **Owner profile:** Female-led, often a nurse practitioner or aesthetician who went independent
- **Size:** 1–5 employees, owner is often still injecting
- **Revenue:** $300K–$2M/year
- **Location targets:** St. Petersburg, Clearwater, Dunedin, Safety Harbor, Largo, Tampa (FL Gulf Coast)

---

## Why They're a Perfect ICP

**High ticket.** Botox, fillers, laser, body sculpting — single visit = $300–$5,000+. One new client covers the cost of the whole system.

**Massive friction.** Consultations, before/afters, trust-building, rebooking sequences, follow-up — most owner-operators are doing this manually or not at all.

**Terrible web presence.** Most med spas have a Wix or Squarespace site built in 2019. No SEO. No content. No schema. Just a booking button and some stock photos.

**AI search is exploding here.** People ask ChatGPT and Gemini "best botox near clearwater" or "lip filler st pete" — and the businesses that show up are the ones with structured, LLM-readable content. Almost none of them have it.

**Daily content is obvious.** Seasonal treatments, new services, city targeting (Tampa, Dunedin, Safety Harbor, Largo), before/after content, treatment explainers — the content calendar writes itself.

**Female-owned.** They understand the value of presentation, trust, and detail. They're not skeptical of AI — they're using it personally already.

**Competitive market.** St. Pete / Clearwater is saturated. The ones who win will own search. Most of them know this and are scared of falling behind.

---

## Their Pain Points
- Relying entirely on Instagram/word of mouth — no sustainable organic traffic
- Google ranking for their business name only, not for services or cities
- Spending on Google Ads with no organic backup
- No system for follow-up or rebooking — revenue leaks constantly
- Owner is the bottleneck for everything: consults, content, comms, onboarding
- Competitors with bigger budgets are pulling ahead

---

## The Pitch Angle
Lead with the reviews — they've earned their reputation through real work. The gap is that their online presence doesn't reflect it. AI search is the urgency hook: the businesses that get structured content live on these platforms now will own their market for years. This isn't a website redesign — it's an infrastructure play.

---

## Content Opportunities (for their site)
- "[City] Botox" / "[City] lip filler" / "[City] body sculpting" — one page per service per city
- Seasonal: summer body prep, holiday Botox, post-summer skin recovery
- Comparison: "Botox vs. Dysport — which is right for you?"
- FAQ pages structured for voice search and LLMs
- Before/after content with treatment context (not just photos)
- Provider bio / trust pages
