## Email 4 — The "What's Actually On These Pages" (Day 9)

Subject: what's on each page

People ask me what I mean by "a page for each service in each city." Fair question.

Here's what a single page looks like — say, "tree removal in [Nearby Town]":

A headline written for that search. Two paragraphs about what tree removal looks like in that area — the types of trees, the common situations, what a homeowner should expect. A local FAQ section — the actual questions people in that town ask. Schema markup that tells Google "this is a tree removal service in [Nearby Town], here's the phone number, here's the service area." Internal links to your other services in that town.

Multiply that across every service you offer and every city you work in.

That's what turns a 3-page site into a 60-page site. And it's what turns "nobody finds us on Google" into "the phone started ringing from places we never advertised."
